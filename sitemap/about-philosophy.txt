Our Philosophy
Breadcrumb: Home > About > Our Philosophy

"P.C.L.S. Inc. draws its philosophy from The Adults Living with an Intellectual Disability Act (ALIDA)."

The ALIDA was developed in 1996 to promote and protect the rights of adults living with a mental disability to meet their basic needs. It reflects a code of conduct inherent in Pulford's policies since its inception in 1986.

Seven Guiding Principles:
1. Adults with intellectual disability are presumed capable of making decisions affecting their lives unless demonstrated otherwise.
2. They should be encouraged to make their own decisions.
3. Support networks should assist in enhancing independence and self-determination.
4. Decision-making assistance should respect privacy, dignity, and use least restrictive means.
5. Substitute decision-making only as last resort.
6. Rights will be protected for full, equal participation in society.
7. Equal rights and freedom from discrimination as all Canadians.
