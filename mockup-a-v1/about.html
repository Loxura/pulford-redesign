About Pulford

Support that begins
with belonging.

We are a Manitoba not-for-profit, established in 1986, built around a simple conviction: people with intellectual and developmental disabilities belong in the full life of their community — not on the edge of it.

Our story

For nearly four decades, Pulford has grown alongside the people it supports. What began as a handful of homes is now a network of day programs, residences, and family supports reaching across Winnipeg, the Interlake, and the Eastman region.

The work has never been about programs for their own sake. It is about a person getting their own keys, a first paycheque, a Tuesday spent doing something they love, a quiet evening at home that feels like home. We measure success in lives, not units of service.

Our approach is person-centred and relationship-first. We listen before we plan, we move at the pace of trust, and we build supports around who someone is and who they want to become.

What guides us
Vision
An equal and inclusive world.
Mission
To bring community together to promote and empower people to live a full life.
Values
