After two years of quiet rebuilding, Pairings with Pulford returned to the Fort Garry Hotel on a snow-bright Saturday — and turned into the most successful fundraising evening in the organization's history.

Two hundred and forty guests filled the ballroom for an evening of food, music and storytelling, raising funds that will go directly into homes, day programs and family supports across Winnipeg, the Interlake and Eastman.

An evening built by the whole community

The night came together through dozens of local partners — restaurants, vintners, musicians and volunteers who gave their time so that every dollar raised could go where it matters most. As board chair Marguerite Delorme put it from the stage:

We don't measure a night like this in tickets sold. We measure it in the doors it opens — a first apartment, a steady job, a Tuesday that finally belongs to the person living it.

Highlights from the evening included:

  • A live raffle that drew the largest single-night response on record
  • Twelve tasting stations hosted by Manitoba chefs and producers
  • A short film featuring three people supported by Pulford, in their own words

Community members and supporters gather at Pairings with Pulford 2026.

Where the funds go next

Proceeds are already being directed toward expanding supported independent living placements and a new respite stream for families. For partnership or sponsorship enquiries, reach out to our team — there's a place for everyone at the next table.
