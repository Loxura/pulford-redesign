Pairings with Pulford 2026: a record-breaking night.

CG Catherine Gates
Executive Director April 15, 2026 4 min read

On Friday, April 10, a room full of daylight and good food kept the evening lively — but the heart of the night was a story told by the people we support. By the time the lights came up, our fifteenth Pairings had become our most generous yet.

Every spring, Pairings with Pulford turns a fundraiser into a celebration. This year we filled the RBC Convention Centre with friends old and new — partners, families, staff, volunteers and the people at the centre of everything we do. There was a full-day polar bear adventure on the block, a $2,500 shopping spree, and a raffle table that drew a crowd all night. But the moment the room went quiet was the moment that mattered most.

One story, one goal

Midway through the evening, one of the people we support took the microphone to talk about something most of us never think twice about: getting from here to there. A medical appointment. A shift at work. A movie with friends. When the only accessible ride is booked, plans don't just shift — they disappear. The goal this year is simple and concrete: a new wheelchair-accessible vehicle.

"It isn't just a van. It's access, independence, dignity and opportunity — the difference between waiting at the door and walking through it."

That framing landed. The live appeal that followed moved faster than any year we can remember, and by the end of the night the vehicle fund was within striking distance of its target. We'll share the final figure once every pledge is in, but it's safe to say the room showed up.

Built by the whole community

An evening like this doesn't happen without a long list of people pulling on the same door. Our thanks go to the partners who made the night possible:

  • South Sherbrook Health Centre & Pharmacy, and Assiniboine Credit Union
  • Maple Leaf Construction, Birchwood Ford and Star Building Materials
  • Pereux Insurance and Manitoba Liquor & Lotteries
  • The dozens of volunteers who set up, served, sold tickets and tore down

And to everyone who bought a ticket, raised a paddle or simply spread the word — thank you. Fifteen years in, the formula hasn't changed: good people, in one room, deciding that doors should open for everyone.

Missed it? You can still help

The goal is still live, and every gift moves it closer. If you'd like to put your name on the vehicle fund, email payment@pulford.ca and note "wheelchair-accessible vehicle," or give online in under a minute.

About the raffle winners

All 24 winning ticket numbers were drawn on the night. Holding a winner? Reach out to Andy at Andy.Russo@pulford.ca to arrange your prize. Thank you to everyone who took part — and good luck next year.
